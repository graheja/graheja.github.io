Garima Raheja

Ayoooo losersssss I’m an atmospheric chemist interested in air pollution, its impacts on community health, and solutions grounded in data and justice. My work is deeply informed by hometown of New Delhi, India and my community in the San Francisco Bay Area. Currently, I’m working towards my PhD at Columbia University, and serving as an Air Quality Fellow for the US State Department and a Community Science Fellow for the AGU Thriving Earth Exchange.

Research

My current PhD work focuses on quantifying emissions of methane, a potent greenhouse gas, and working with the NY State Energy Research & Development Authority to mitigate its release and its impacts. Click below to learn about my ongoing and previous projects.

Connecting with Communities

Environmental justice begins with community-based, community-driven research. I work with groups across the country and across the world to bring justice into practice.

Education

Working with teachers and students, I’ve developed workshops, curriculums, and best practices for bringing climate science and environmental justice into the classroom. Click below to learn more, and access open-source lesson plans.
